- 2026-07-23 [planwright] Config-resolver family divergence: config-get.sh documents (and implements) that a malformed adopter/machine-local overlay degrades to the NEXT LOWER LAYER. But the resolve-config-knob.sh / resolve-dispatch-backend.sh family degrades a malformed adopter/machine-local VALUE straight to the CORE default (neutralizing every overlay layer), per the task REQ-E1.4 "degrade to the core default" wording. Observable effect: repo-tracked sets a valid value, machine-local sets a malformed one -> the resolver jumps to core, silently bypassing the valid repo-tracked value that config-gets own next-lower-layer rule would have used. The two are consistent within their own families but mutually inconsistent and undocumented as such. Consider harmonizing the degrade target across config-get and the resolve-*-knob family, or documenting the file-level (config-get) vs value-level (resolver) distinction explicitly.
